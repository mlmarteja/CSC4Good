Analysis

With this data, we are examining how a person’s insurance costs are affected by several variables, and we are trying to determine if there are any variables leading to people being unfairly charged. Linear regression will be our primary way of determining how insurance costs are impacted by a factor. The two factors that are used to analyze this relationship are the line of best fit and the coefficient of determination (also known as R-Squared).

The use of regression showed that many of the categories proved to be unhelpful when analyzed for their coefficient of determination. While region had an R Squared value of about .43 and sex had an R Squared value of about .33, age, bmi, and children had an R Squared values between -0 and -1, and smoking had an R Squared value higher than 1. When a variable’s R Squared value is not between the range of 0 and 1, it means that the data provided does not have accurate explaining power for the dependent variable when analyzed using linear regression.
While the coefficient of determination did not provide conclusive explaining power for some of the categories, the line of best fit for each variable is able to show us how insurance costs are impacted. For age, it is clear that insurance costs increase as one grows older. For sex, it appears that women are charged a minor amount less compared to men. With bmi, it is apparent that insurance cost increases along with a person’s weight, especially once the bmi rises above 30. Children seem to also have a negative impact on insurance costs, raising a person’s cost as the amount of children they have increases. For smoking, it is obvious that smokers are charged more for insurance than people who do not smoke. Region can not accurately be analyzed by a line of best fit, due to the fact that it is not truly a linear variable.
