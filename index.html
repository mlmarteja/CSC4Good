Introduction

Insurance is a contract that receives financial protection or reimbursement against losses. In other words, insurance is where everyone in a group chips in to help any member of the group with a loss. When deciding whether to invest in insurance, it is important to understand the risks that comes with it. Although insurance is beneficial, it has its problems. Insurance’s assigned risks put people into a category, thus many unfortunate individuals are forced pay a higher price.

In this project, we are looking to help indivuals analyze health inruance costs. Because helth inruance is important despite age and health, most people have it because no one is pone to a medeical emergency. However, it is expensive. For this project, we decided to work with a dataset of a person’s health insurance cost along with their age, sex, bmi, number of children, region, and whether or not they’re a smoker. We then planned to analyze this data using linear regression to determine which of these factors have the most effect on a person’s insurance costs. Using this data, we could see if there are any demographics being unfairly charged for their insurance, such as the elderly, parents, or smokers.

We have created a tutorial that will walk users through a data sience pipeline: gathering, cleaning, statistical anlysis, and visualization. This project will illustrate how we utilize the data in linear regression to illustrate insurance costs through different demographics
