Introduction

Insurance is a contract that is represented by a policy. It provides individuals to recive finanfical reimbursment or protection agansts financial loss. However, insruance cost can be unfair for many individuals. For example is health insurance. This can be costly despite a person's age or health. As a result, many patients would have to pay high out-of-pocket cost. Just like health insurnace, other insurances can be costly.

For this project, we decided to work with a dataset of a person’s insurance cost along with their age, sex, bmi, number of children, region, and whether or not they’re a smoker. We then planned to analyze this data using linear regression to determine which of these factors have the most effect on a person’s insurance costs. Using this data, we could see if there are any demographics being unfairly charged for their insurance, such as the elderly, parents, or smokers.

In this final project, we have created a tutorial that will walk users through a data sience pipeline: gathering, cleaning, statistical anlysis, and visualization.
